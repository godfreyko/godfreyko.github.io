Project Management

CIT 380

Week 1 Teaching Notes

Outcomes

By the end of the lesson, each student will:

  1. Feel attachment to and explain attributes of their classmates
  2. Recite and explain some of the fundamental aspects of PM
  3. Appreciate the importance of PM principles in professional and everyday life

Before the Lesson Begins

  1. Read the Instructor Course Overview
  2. Send out an email the first week of class to let your class know that they will hit the ground running. Include the following:
    1. Information about yourself. Where you are from, your educational background, etc.
    2. Reminder to purchase course textbook if they do not want to read it online. (students have a reading assignment in Week 01).
    3. Students should participate in an Introduction discussion board. Encourage students to make their initial post earlier in the week; this will make it easier to respond to at least four other students' posts.
  3. Post a story in the announcements about a project that went terribly wrong. See the two case studies for an example, but make yours much shorter.
  4. Read the course material for this week. The more familiar you are with the course and its contents, the better prepared you are to answer student questions.
  5. Adjust W01 due dates for students who add late.

During the Lesson

  1. Be very active in the icebreaker to the point of over-participating. This will set a tone for interaction that the students will notice.
  2. Inform students of your office hours.
  3. Answer student questions via email, office hours, and Announcements discussion.
